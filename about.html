About This Project

Making Genetics Click

Decoding Genetics is a visual learning hub built to turn dense, technical genetics concepts into animations anyone can follow — no science degree required.

The Motivation

Why Build This?

Genetics is often taught through dense textbook diagrams and jargon-heavy language that can make an inherently visual, dynamic subject feel abstract and inaccessible. DNA, proteins, and molecular interactions are physical, moving systems — but they're rarely explained that way.

This hub was created to close that gap. By pairing accurate scientific content with cinematic 3D and 2D animation, complex ideas like antisense oligonucleotide (ASO) therapies or the relationship between genetic variants and disease become something you can actually see happen — making them easier to understand, remember, and explain to others.

What You'll Take Away

Key Learning Objectives

01

Understand Core Molecular Biology

Build a solid foundation in DNA, RNA, and protein structure, and how these molecules work together.

02

Connect Variants to Disease

Learn how genetic variation arises and how it can influence protein function and health outcomes.

03

Explore Emerging Therapies

Understand how antisense oligonucleotides (ASOs) work, and where their limitations lie.

04

Think Visually About Science

Develop the ability to picture molecular processes in three dimensions, not just as static diagrams.
